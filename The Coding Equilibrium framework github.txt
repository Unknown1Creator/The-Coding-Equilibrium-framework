The Coding Equilibrium framework quantifies software operational pressure through three orthogonal dimensions: **Friction**, **Volatility**, and **Blockage**, each modeled with statistically grounded, context-aware metrics. These are aggregated into a unified diagnostic score, the **Equilibrium Score (EQ)**, and emitted alongside a **Pressure Vector (F, V, B)** for actionable triage.



---

## I. **Adaptive Baseline Scoring (ABS) — Friction Signature**

Let $ x_t $ denote the resource consumption (time or memory) of execution $ t $ for a given code path, with $ n $ total executions. Define $ \mu_t $ and $ \sigma_t $ as the exponentially smoothed running mean and standard deviation of resource consumption:

$$
\mu_t = \alpha \cdot x_t + (1 - \alpha) \cdot \mu_{t-1}
$$
$$
\sigma_t = \beta \cdot |x_t - \mu_t| + (1 - \beta) \cdot \sigma_{t-1}
$$

where $ \alpha, \beta \in (0,1) $ are smoothing constants (typically $ \alpha = 0.1 $, $ \beta = 0.05 $).

The **Z-score** for execution $ t $ is:

$$
z_t = \frac{|x_t - \mu_t|}{\sigma_t}
$$

The **Friction Signature** $ F_t $ is a binary indicator:

$$
F_t = \begin{cases}
1.0 & \text{if } |z_t| > k \\
0.0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$

where $ k $ is the allowable variance threshold — typically 3 in production, or dynamically set to the 99th percentile of historical $ z $-scores.

To normalize for aggregation:

$$
F_{\text{norm}} = \frac{F_t}{\max(F_t)} \quad \text{(if } F_t \in \{0,1\} \text{, then } F_{\text{norm}} = F_t \text{)}
$$

## II. **Uncovered Path Entropy (UPE) — Volatility Signature** ? **UPDATED**

Let $ P_{\text{unseen}} $ denote the set of untested logic paths in a module. Each path $ p \in P_{\text{unseen}} $ has a nesting depth $ d_p $. The weight of path $ p $ is:

$$
W_p = \begin{cases}
0.1 \cdot 2^{d_p} & \text{if last observed } > 30 \text{ days} \\
2^{d_p} & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$

The total **Uncovered Path Entropy** is:

$$
\text{UPE} = \sum_{p \in P_{\text{unseen}}} W_p
$$

The **Volatility Signature Velocity** $ \nu $ is the rate of change over a sliding window $ \Delta t $ (e.g., 5 minutes):

$$
\nu = \frac{\Delta \text{UPE}}{\Delta t}
$$

The **Volatility Signature** $ V $ is computed as:

$$
V = \min\left(1.0, \nu \cdot \text{scale\_factor}\right)
$$

where

$$
\text{scale\_factor} = \begin{cases}
1.0 & \text{if } \text{UPE} > 100 \\
0.1 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$

> ? **Note**: This ensures that **even low UPE systems can detect volatility early** — the scaling factor is applied *before* the min(1.0) normalization.

## III. **Stall-to-Work Ratio (SWR) — Blockage Signature**

Let $ \text{work}_t $ denote the time spent in active computation, and $ \text{stall}_t $ denote the time spent in blocked states (I/O, locks, waits). The **Stall-to-Work Ratio** is:

$$
\text{SWR} = \frac{\text{stall}_t}{\text{work}_t + \text{stall}_t}
$$

The **Blockage Signature** $ B $ is a binary flag:

$$
B = \begin{cases}
1.0 & \text{if } \text{SWR} > 0.8 \\
0.0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$

To normalize for aggregation:

$$
B_{\text{norm}} = \min(1.0, \text{SWR})
$$

---

## IV. **Equilibrium Score (EQ) — Composite Pressure Metric**

The **Equilibrium Score** aggregates the normalized pressure signatures using weighted components. Context-aware weights $ w_1 $ and $ w_2 $ are applied based on environment:

$$
w_1 = \begin{cases}
0.5 & \text{if environment = CI} \\
0.3 & \text{if environment = Staging} \\
0.2 & \text{if environment = Production}
\end{cases}
$$

$$
w_2 = \begin{cases}
0.2 & \text{if environment = CI} \\
0.4 & \text{if environment = Staging} \\
0.6 & \text{if environment = Production}
\end{cases}
$$

The final **Equilibrium Score** is:

$$
\text{EQ} = F_{\text{norm}} + (V \cdot w_1) + (B_{\text{norm}} \cdot w_2)
$$

> ? **Note**: All components are normalized to [0, 1] or [0, 100], and EQ ? [0, 1 + w_1 + w_2] ? typically [0, 1.5] for full range.

---

## V. **Pressure Vector — Diagnostic Signature**

The **Pressure Vector** $ \mathbf{P} = (F, V, B) $ is emitted alongside EQ for engineering triage:

- $ F \in [0,1] $ — Friction: computational waste relative to baseline
- $ V \in [0,1] $ — Volatility: latent fragility from untested paths
- $ B \in [0,1] $ — Blockage: contention or external stalls

The vector is interpreted as follows:

| Signature | Threshold | Engineering Action |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| $ F > 0.7 $ | High Friction | Optimize loops, reduce compute waste |
| $ V > 0.5 $ | High Volatility | Write tests for uncovered paths |
| $ B > 0.7 $ | High Blockage | Reduce locks, optimize I/O |

## VI. **Supporting Enhancements**

### A. Dynamic Variance Threshold (k)

$$
k = \text{percentile}_{99}(z_t) \quad \text{or} \quad k = \text{median}(z_t) + 3 \cdot \text{IQR}
$$

### B. Exponential Smoothing Parameters

$$
\mu_t = \alpha x_t + (1 - \alpha) \mu_{t-1} \\
\sigma_t = \beta |x_t - \mu_t| + (1 - \beta) \sigma_{t-1}
$$

with $ \alpha = 0.1 $, $ \beta = 0.05 $.
